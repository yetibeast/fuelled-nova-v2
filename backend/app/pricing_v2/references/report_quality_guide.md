## What Good Report Sections Look Like

These excerpts from real Fuelled valuations show the quality standard. Match this depth and specificity in every valuation.

### Executive Summary
Open with client context, equipment description, and technical specifics. Not generic — ground it in real hardware:
> "ARC Resources has requested a fair market valuation for four (4) electric fresh water transfer pump packages currently surplus to operational requirements. Each package is a complete, self-contained unit consisting of a Pioneer centrifugal pump rated at 20,000 m³/day at 200 PSIG, driven by a WEG 700 HP electric motor with VFD, housed in a purpose-built Class I Division II building."

### Market Context
Tie regulatory or demand drivers to buyer behavior with specific price differentials:
> "The VRU market in Western Canada is currently favorable for sellers: federal methane regulations require 75% reduction by 2030, and many operators are actively installing VRUs at existing sites. A used unit at $35K–$55K is significantly cheaper than a new package at $200K+ with 6–12 month lead times."

For US equipment, reference the relevant basin (Permian, Eagle Ford, Marcellus), US natural gas pricing, and state-level regulatory context — NOT WCSB/Montney unless the equipment is in Canada.

### Condition Assessment
Frame competing value drivers with cost implications:
> "This unit is unused (zero hours, original components) but 17 years old. These factors pull in opposite directions: zero hours mean no mechanical wear, yet elastomers, seals, and gaskets degrade over time even without operation. A buyer will want a pre-commissioning inspection ($3K–$8K) before relying on this unit."

### Comparable Analysis
Show direct evidence, establish range, demonstrate convergence between methods:
> "The two strongest comparables are the CAT G3306NA / Gemini H302 at $50,000 (1998, Vulcan AB) and the CAT G3306TA / GE FE332C at $40,000 (1988, Forestburg AB). Market range: $25,000–$45,000 for a 3306-driven 2-stage sweet package. This aligns with the RCN-based FMV of $27,500, confirming the valuation."

Remember: asking prices are 80–90% of actual transaction values. Flag when comps are from the same operator or location (strongest basis). Always include the source and listing reference.

### Cost Considerations
Quantify transport, re-certification, and overhaul costs:
> "Each package weighs ~32,000 lbs, requiring oversize transport permitting. Typical move costs within Alberta: $15,000–$30,000/unit. Registered equipment with current data sheets commands a 10% premium; equipment without documentation may require re-certification, reducing effective value by 10–15%."

### Marketing Guidance
Actionable, specific, grounded in market context:
> "Lead with 'unused / zero hours' and 'NACE sour service rated' in the listing title. Highlight that new VRU packages are $200K+ with 6–12 month lead times. A buyer gets immediate availability at a fraction of new cost."

### Scenario Analysis
Show the economics that drive buyer decisions:
> "Frame/valve overhaul for a JGP: $40K–$60K. Engine overhaul on a G3306NA: $25K–$40K. A buyer with in-house overhaul capability could achieve this for $30K–$50K, making an as-is purchase at $25K–$35K economically viable."

### Equipment Context
Acknowledge rarity or preference shifts:
> "The JGP frame is uncommon in new builds — most packagers now start at the JGJ/2 or JGC/2. This RCN represents a functionally equivalent new package, not an identical JGP replacement."

### Key Rules
- Every comparable must have a source and listing reference
- Always state the currency (CAD or USD) — match to equipment location
- Note what data is missing and how it affects the valuation
- Equipment-specific assumptions, not boilerplate
- If two methods converge, say so explicitly — it strengthens the conclusion
